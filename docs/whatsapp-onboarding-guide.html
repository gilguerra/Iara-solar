Meta BSP vs Direct Cloud API — Important Distinction

Before applying for anything, it's critical to understand the difference between becoming a Meta Business Solution Provider (BSP) and simply using the WhatsApp Cloud API directly as a business.

Direct Cloud API BSP (Business Solution Provider)
Who it's for Any business using WhatsApp for their own operations Companies that resell WhatsApp access to other businesses
What you can do Run your own numbers Manage numbers on behalf of clients
Approval Self-serve, fast Application process, several weeks
Cost Free to apply Usually requires revenue commitment
Bottom line for Iara Solar You are not reselling raw API access — you are offering a SaaS product that uses WhatsApp as the channel. This puts you in the Tech Provider category, not BSP. You don't need BSP status.

What You Actually Need: Tech Provider

Meta has a lighter track called Tech Provider (formerly "Solution Provider") which lets you build products on top of WhatsApp and use Embedded Signup to connect client numbers without becoming a full BSP. This is the correct path for Iara Solar.

Business & Legal Requirements

  • Registered legal entity — CNPJ in Brazil is sufficient
  • Meta Business Account in good standing
  • Agreement to WhatsApp Business Platform Terms of Service

Technical Requirements

  • Working app registered in Meta Developers console
  • Webhook endpoint that passes Meta's verification challenge (GET + hub.challenge)
  • SSL certificate on your webhook URL (already handled by Traefik + Let's Encrypt)
  • Embedded Signup flow implemented on your landing page
No minimum revenue or customer base The Tech Provider path is self-serve. There are no minimum thresholds required to apply.

The Full Onboarding Flow

Once approved, this is how an Iara Solar customer goes from landing page to a live WhatsApp number:

Landing page → Checkout (Stripe) → Embedded Signup (Meta OAuth)
→ Your backend auto-provisions tenant → Number is live in minutes
1
Landing page + payment Company fills a form (name, CNPJ, email, phone number they want to use), pays via Stripe subscription. On payment_intent.succeeded webhook, the onboarding sequence is triggered.
2
Meta Embedded Signup A Meta-provided OAuth button embedded on your page. The solar company:
  1. Clicks "Connect WhatsApp"
  2. Logs into their Facebook/Meta Business account
  3. Selects or registers their phone number
  4. Grants your app permission to send/receive on their behalf

Meta returns an access_token + phone_number_id for that tenant:

{
  "phone_number_id": "123456789",
  "waba_id": "987654321",
  "access_token": "EAAxx..."
}
3
Backend auto-provisioning Your /onboarding/complete endpoint:
  1. Creates a tenant row in the database
  2. Generates an API key (scripts/generate-api-key.js logic)
  3. Stores phone_number_id + access_token in tenant config
  4. Registers the webhook subscription for that WABA with Meta
  5. Sends a welcome message to the company's test number
4
Number is live Any WhatsApp message sent to that number hits Meta → your n8n webhook → Iara Solar flow → reply goes back automatically.

Application Process (Step by Step)

1
Create a Meta Business Account Go to business.facebook.com and verify your business with your CNPJ document.
2
Create an App in Meta Developers Go to developers.facebook.com → My Apps → Create App → Type: Business. Add the "WhatsApp" product to the app.
3
Get approved for WhatsApp Business Platform Submit your business use case. Brazil-based businesses are generally approved within 1–5 business days. You get a test number immediately while approval processes.
4
Enable Embedded Signup Apply for the whatsapp_business_management permission. This allows your app to manage other companies' numbers. Requires a short review (~1 week).
5
Go Live Submit your app for Meta review with a screencast of your Embedded Signup flow. Meta verifies you are not misusing access.

Pre-Application Checklist

  • CNPJ registered legal entity
  • Business website live (landing page is sufficient)
  • Privacy Policy URL publicly accessible
  • Terms of Service URL publicly accessible
  • Clear description of use case prepared (see below)
  • SSL-enabled webhook URL live and responding
Suggested use case description "Solar installation companies use our SaaS platform to automate customer quote requests via WhatsApp. When a homeowner contacts a solar company's WhatsApp number, our system collects energy consumption data, calculates the required solar system size, and returns a personalized equipment quote — all automatically."

Brazil-Specific Notes

  • Brazil is WhatsApp's largest market — Meta has a dedicated team there
  • Review processes are generally faster for Brazilian companies
  • Meta is familiar with SaaS products built on the platform in Brazil
  • Reference companies that started as Tech Providers before becoming full BSPs: Zenvia, Take Blip, Botmaker
